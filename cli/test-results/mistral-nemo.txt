QUESTION 5: What are the specific requirements for cosmetic product facility registration and product listing under the Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act of 2022?

ANSWER: Based on the provided context, here are the specific requirements for cosmetic product facility registration and product listing under the Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act of 2022 (MoCRA):

**Cosmetic Product Facility Registration:**

1. **Initial Registration:**
   - Every person owning or operating a facility manufacturing/processing cosmetics for U.S. distribution must register each facility by December 29, 2023.
   - New facilities engaging in such activities after December 29, 2022, must register within 60 days of starting operations or by February 27, 2024.

2. **Amended Registration:**
   - Facilities must update their registration within 60 days of any changes to required information.
   - Changes resulting in cancellation of the registration also require an amended registration.

3. **Renewal of Registration:**
   - Facilities must renew their registrations biennially (every two years).
   - FDA offers abbreviated renewal for facilities with no updates since the last submission.

4. **Responsibility:**
   - The facility owner/operator or any responsible person whose products are manufactured/processed at such facility can submit the registration.
   - Contract manufacturers can also submit registrations on behalf of their clients.

**Cosmetic Product Listing:**

QUESTION 7: What are the FDA's recommendations for ensuring the safety of cosmetic products that contain nanomaterials?

ANSWER: The FDA recommends considering several factors to ensure the safety of cosmetic products containing nanomaterials:

1. **Physicochemical Characteristics**:
   - Agglomeration and size distribution under testing conditions and in the final product.
   - Impurities present.

2. **Potential Routes of Exposure**:
   - Potential for aggregation and agglomeration in the final product.
   - Dosimetry for in vitro and in vivo toxicology studies.

3. **Toxicological Data**:
   - In vitro and in vivo toxicological data on nanomaterial ingredients and their impurities.
   - Dermal penetration, potential inhalation, irritation (skin and eye), sensitization studies.
   - Mutagenicity/genotoxicity studies.

4. **Safety Assessment**:
   - Addressing unique properties and biological effects of nanomaterials during safety evaluations.
   - Considering routes of exposure, uptake, absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion.

5. **Testing Methods**:
   - Phototoxicity testing via 3T3 NRPT for UV absorbing substances.
   - Human/pig skin in a diffusion cell for dermal absorption.
   - BCOP and ICE for ocular irritation.
   - Genotoxicity testing using a battery of recommended tests covering gene mutation, structural, and numerical aberrations.

6. **In vivo Studies**:
   - May be more suitable for nanomaterials with limited solubility properties.

7. **Consultation**:
   - Encouraged to discuss test methods and data needed to substantiate the product's safety with the FDA.

   - For cosmetic recalls, also provide:
     - The correct and incorrect label(s), description(s), and formulation(s) if the recall is due to a label issue.

QUESTION 18: What are common mistakes with non compliance when starting a cosmetic business?

ANSWER: Based on the provided context, here are some common mistakes related to non-compliance when starting a cosmetic business:

1. **Not Registering or Listing Facilities/Products**: Failing to register facilities that manufacture or process cosmetics and failing to list products as required by section 607 of the FD&C Act.
2. **Exemption Misunderstandings**: Incorrectly assuming exemption from registration and listing requirements due to being a "small business" under section 612, without meeting all the criteria for exemption.
3. **Not Including Required Information**: Not providing complete information required by sections 607(b)(2)(D) and (E), such as the name and address of the facility, the name and address of the responsible person, and a description of the cosmetic product.
4. **Importer Non-compliance**: Importers not registering or listing if they meet the definition of a facility or responsible person under section 604.
5. **Mislabeling Products**: Not providing accurate labeling, including directions for use, which could lead to products falling under section 612(b)(4) and requiring registration and listing.
6. **Not Complying with Exemptions**: For example, a laboratory that performs testing on cosmetic products used for research and development may mistakenly believe they need to register due to misunderstanding the exemption criteria.
