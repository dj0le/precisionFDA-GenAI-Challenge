QUESTION 2: What are the key recommendations for good manufacturing practices (GMP) in the cosmetic industry?

ANSWER: The key recommendation for good manufacturing practices (GMP) in the cosmetic industry is as follows:

1. Ensure that personnel supervising or performing cosmetics manufacturing or control have education, training, and/or experience to perform their assigned functions.
2. Personnel should wear clean clothing appropriate for the duties they perform and necessary protective apparatus (for example, uniforms, gloves, safety glasses, and hair restraints).
3. Eating food, dripping beverages, or using tobacco products should be restricted to designated areas away from production, storage, and product control areas.
4. Raw materials are identified, stored, examined, tested, inventoried, handled, and controlled to ensure they conform to approved standards and specifications.
5. Sampled and tested for conformity with specification and to ensure the absence of filth, microorganisms, and other adulterants prior to processing or usage (animals and vegetable origin materials and those produced by cold processing methods should be reviewed for filth and/or microorganism contaminations).
6. Properly identify and control raw materials to ensure they conform to approved standards and specifications.
7. Ensure that only authorized personnel have access into production, storage, and product control areas.
8. Conduct regular inspections of the manufacturing facility to ensure compliance with GMPs.

QUESTION 5: What are the specific requirements for cosmetic product facility registration and product listing under the Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act of 2022?

ANSWER: The specific requirements for cosmestic product facility registration and product listing under the Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act of 2022 are:

1. Initial Registration: Every person that, on December 29, 2022, owns or operate a facility that engages in the manufacturing or processing of a cosmestic product for distribution in the United States must register each facility no later than December 29, 2023 (section 607(a)(1) of the FD&C Act).

2. Registration: Every person that owns or operate a facility that first engages, after December 29, 2022, in manufacturing or processing of a cosmestic product for distribution in the United States must register such facility within 60 days of first engaging in such activity or by February 27, 2024 (section 607(a)(1) of the FD&C Act).

3. Amended Registration: Every person who is required to register a facility must renew such registration biennially (i.e., every two years) as required under section 607(a)(2) of the FD&C Act.

4. Facility Registration: Every person that owns or operates a facility that engages in the manufacturing or processing of a cosmestic product for distribution in the United States must update their registration within 60 days of any changes to the information required for registration (section 607(a)(4) of the FD&C Act).

5. Renewal: Every person who is required to register a facility may submit updates for a cosmestic product facility registration by submitting a renewal application within the timeframe specified in section 607(a)(2) of the FD&C Act.

QUESTION 16: I'm starting a small cosmetic company, what should I do first to comply with FDA cosmetic guidance?

ANSWER: To comply with FDA cosmetic guidance, you need to register your facility and submit product listing information under section 607 of the Food Drug & Cosmetic Act. The responsible person must submit a cosmestic product listing or ensure such submission is made for each cosmestic product they manufacture or process on behalf of more than one responsible person. If you are an owner or operator of a contract manufacturing facility, you do not need to register your facility if the responsible person submits the product listing. The listed product should be identified as both a drug and a cosmetic in the drug listing submission.

QUESTION 17: What are the best practices for registering a new facility for cosmetic manufacturing?

ANSWER: The best practices for registering a new facility for cosmestic manufacturing include:

1. Determine the appropriate registration category or categories for your product(s) based on their intended use and market demand.

2. Obtain all necessary documentation, including facility registration number(s), from the applicable regulatory agency (e.g., FDA).

3. Submit a complete and accurate application with all required information, including the facility's name, address, and contact information.

4. Ensure that the facility meets all relevant safety standards and requirements for manufacturing cosmetics.

5. Provide detailed information about the facility's operations, including the number of employees, equipment, and facilities used to manufacture and process cosmetic products.

6. Submit updates to the FDA as necessary, such as changes in product formulation or ingredients, to ensure that the facility remains compliant with regulatory requirements.

7. Obtain any necessary approvals from local, state, or federal authorities before commencing operations.

8. Maintain accurate and up-to-date records of all manufacturing activities, including product testing, batch records, and quality control procedures.

9. Follow all applicable safety guidelines and protocols for handling, storing, and disposing of hazardous materials in the facility.

10. Ensure that all employees are trained on proper safety practices and procedures for working with cosmetic products.

11. Regularly inspect the facility to ensure that it is operating safely and efficiently, and address any issues promptly.

12. Maintain a clean and organized workspace to minimize the risk of contamination and ensure proper storage of materials and equipment.

13. Follow all applicable regulatory requirements for labeling, packaging, and advertising of cosmetic products.

14. Continuously monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of the facility's safety measures to ensure that they are adequate and up-to-date with changing industry standards.
