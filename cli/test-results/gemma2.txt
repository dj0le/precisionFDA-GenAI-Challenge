QUESTION 7: What are the FDA's recommendations for ensuring the safety of cosmetic products that contain nanomaterials?

ANSWER: The FDA recommends a comprehensive safety assessment for cosmetic products containing nanomaterials. This includes:

* **Thorough Characterization:**  A detailed description of the nanomaterial, including its physicochemical properties, size distribution, agglomeration potential, and any impurities present.
* **Toxicological Evaluation:**  Addressing potential routes of exposure (dermal, inhalation), uptake, absorption, metabolism, and excretion of the nanomaterial. This should involve both in vitro and in vivo studies to assess toxicity endpoints like irritation, sensitization, mutagenicity/genotoxicity.

**Specific Testing Recommendations:**

* Phototoxicity testing using 3T3 NRPT
* Human/pig skin diffusion cell for dermal absorption
* Bovine Corneal Opacity and Permeability (BCOP) and Isolated Chicken Eye (ICE) for ocular irritation
* Genotoxicity testing battery covering gene mutation, structural and numerical aberrations.  Consider the nanomaterial's properties when interpreting genotoxicity results.
* In vivo studies may be more suitable for nanomaterials with limited solubility.

**Additional Considerations:**

* **Unique Properties:** Acknowledge that nanomaterials can exhibit novel properties different from larger-scale counterparts.
* **Traditional Method Applicability:** Recognize that traditional safety testing methods may not fully apply to nanomaterials due to their unique characteristics.
* **Data Substantiation:** Provide sufficient data, including short-term and long-term toxicity studies, to support the safety of the product.


The FDA encourages manufacturers to engage with them early in the process to discuss specific test methods and data requirements for ensuring the safety of cosmetic products containing nanomaterials.
